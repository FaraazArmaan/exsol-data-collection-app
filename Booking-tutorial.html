1. The simple picture

What Booking is for

It lets a customer reserve real time.

A customer chooses what they need, a person or place if relevant, and an available time. The system checks the business rules before confirming the visit.

It works for salons, consultants, clinics, studios, rental businesses, classes, and any business that needs to protect a time slot.

It protects the business schedule.

The module checks working hours, staff shifts and leave, service duration, buffer time, booking lead time, and existing reservations. A slot is not shown as available just because it looks empty on a calendar.

When staff are selected, Workforce is the source of truth for their availability.

Useful terms: A service is what the customer receives, such as a consultation or haircut. A visit is the complete customer reservation. A visit can contain more than one service. A booking rule is a business decision, such as how late someone may cancel.
You will not see a generic “Resources” area. The software uses a technical capacity record internally, but the client-facing labels are meaningful: Doctors, Stylists, Rooms, Vehicles, or Equipment. Booking Setup chooses the correct language for your business.

Click-by-click: Booking Setup

1

Open Booking from the platform navigation and select Booking Setup.

2

Answer Who do customers book with? Choose a named team member, any available team member, or no person at all.

3

Answer What can be booked? Choose appointments, rooms/spaces, equipment/assets, or a combination.

4

Answer whether a booking also needs a room, equipment, both, or neither. Example: a doctor consultation may need both a doctor and examination room.

5

Choose availability source. Pick Workforce shifts and leave for team-based businesses. Pick manual business hours only if no Workforce schedule is used yet.

6

Connect services using clear prompts: “This service can be performed by...”, “This service needs...”, and “This booking uses...”. Save the setup.

What happens when you save? Booking stores the answers, creates sensible default rules, and chooses the client-facing capacity labels. You can reopen Booking Setup later to change the answers; the configuration is editable rather than a one-time wizard.

Then add services

Open Services, select Add service, enter the service name, duration, price, and availability. Repeat for every customer-facing offering. A service must be active before customers can select it.

Then connect capacity

Use the contextual pages created by setup, such as Team Availability, Doctors, Rooms & Spaces, or Equipment. Do not think in generic technical categories; think about what must be free for the booking to happen.

4. Owner tutorial

Publish Booking before customers can use the storefront.

Publishing is deliberate. Completing Booking Setup does not automatically expose your schedule online. Booking stays private until an authorised owner turns on Online booking in Storefront. This prevents customers seeing an unfinished service list or unreliable availability.

Click-by-click: publish your storefront

1

Finish Booking Setup and create at least one active service.

2

Open Storefront from the workspace navigation.

3

Find Online booking. It stays unavailable until Booking Setup is complete and a service is active.

4

Turn Online booking on, then review the displayed customer address.

5

Make a test booking as a customer before sharing the address publicly. Online ordering is controlled separately in the same Storefront page.

One business site, two independent offers

Your public site can show Shop, Book, both, or neither. Publishing Booking never publishes ecommerce, and publishing ecommerce never opens Booking.

When you need to pause bookings

Turn Online booking off from Storefront. Existing customers can still use their secure management link; new visitors cannot view services, availability or make a new booking.

5. Customer tutorial

How a customer makes and manages a booking

Where customers start: the branded public booking storefront is https://your-exsol-domain/book/your-business-slug. Replace your-business-slug with the business workspace slug. This is an anonymous customer page, not a workspace page: customers do not need an ExSol login. It is visible only after the owner has published Booking.

Older shared links using /c/your-business-slug/book remain a compatibility path. Use /book/your-business-slug in new materials because it is the branded storefront route and supports the shared Shop and Book navigation.

Click-by-click: make a booking

1

Open the business booking link. The current address format is /book/your-business-slug. If the business also sells products, use the Book link in its public navigation rather than the Shop link.

2

Select one or more services. The system creates one visit with separate service lines, so the customer does not need separate bookings.

3

Select a team member when the business offers that choice, or select “any available” if offered.

4

Choose a date and an offered time. Only slots that pass the business availability checks appear.

5

Enter contact details and accept any required consent. Review the appointment summary and applicable policy.

6

Choose pay at venue when that option is offered and confirm. The customer sees a confirmation with the visit details. Card, UPI, deposits and refunds require the separate Payments module before they can be collected online.

What the customer sees after booking

The confirmation records the visit reference, selected services, time, and status. A calendar file can be used to add the visit to a personal calendar.

For a business that permits it, the customer can use the management link to view the booking, choose another open slot, or cancel. The rules shown are the rules that applied when the visit was created.

Important: Cancellation and rescheduling are allowed only inside the limits set by the business. A change frees the old reserved time after the new time has been safely accepted.

2

Review the customer, all services, scheduled time, assigned person/place, payment status, and policy snapshot.

3

When the customer arrives, select Check in. This records an event in the booking history.

8. Payments, honestly explained

Booking records payment state. Provider collection is a separate phase.

Available now

Pay at venue and cash record

A booking can stay cash pending until the customer pays. When cash is received, an authorised team member records it from the booking detail. The history shows that action.

This separates the appointment state from payment state. A person can be booked and confirmed while their cash payment is still pending.

Next phase: Payments module

Card, UPI, deposits, refunds

The Payments module will securely connect a provider such as Razorpay. It will confirm online payment, handle failures, deposits, refunds, and reconciliation. Booking will consume that confirmed result; it must not assume an online payment succeeded.

Do not enter payment-provider keys in Booking screens or documents. They belong in the protected Payments configuration.

9. Data, permissions, and connected modules

What is stored, and who needs it

System Booking reads from it Booking provides back
Workforce Team members, working shifts, and leave. Scheduled workload and visit assignment context.
Customer / CRM Customer contact details where connected. Visit history, cancellations, no-shows, and service interest.
Payments Confirmed online payment, refund, and failure results. Amount due, deposit requirement, booking reference, and payment purpose.
Notifications Delivery status for confirmations and reminders. Confirmation, change, cancellation, and reminder requests.
Reporting / finance Optional payment and sales aggregation. Service demand, attendance, payment state, and operational history.

What Booking stores

It stores the visit, individual service lines, scheduled times, capacity reservations, selected team member/place if applicable, policy snapshot, payment status, and lifecycle events. Storing each service line separately lets one visit include several services without losing capacity accuracy.

Permissions and privacy

Owners configure Booking. Authorised staff operate bookings according to role permissions. Customers should see only their own booking through a secure management path. Staff should only access customer information needed to deliver and manage the service.

Technical note for administrators: database and API names may use a generic internal term for capacity records. This does not control the client interface. The configured display type and label determine whether people see Doctors, Stylists, Vehicles, Rooms, or Equipment.

4

Set booking, cancellation, rescheduling, deposit, buffer, and no-show rules.

5

6

Make one test booking as a customer, then check it in and complete it as staff.

7

Before enabling online payment

1

Finish the separate Payments module setup and provider verification.

2

Decide which services require a deposit, full prepayment, or payment at venue.

3

Test success, failure, cancellation, and refund paths with the payment provider test environment.

4

Train staff: only record cash after receiving it, and never mark an online payment paid manually without verified evidence.
