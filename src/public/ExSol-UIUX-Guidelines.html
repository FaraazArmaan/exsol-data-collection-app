EXSOL · SHARED PRODUCT REFERENCE

UI/UX Guidelines

This is the living design-and-system decision record for every ExSol module. It explains the rule, why it exists, and what it means for desktop, phone, accessibility, data, permissions, and implementation. Modules may extend a rule when their domain genuinely needs it; they may not silently contradict it.

One shared truthDesktop and phone may use different layouts, but they must use the same records, business rules, permissions, and audit history.
Adapt, do not duplicatePhone can have a focused workflow when the role’s job is different; it must not become a disconnected second product.
Preview before implementationEvery future decision must update realistic desktop and phone previews side by side, including relevant loading, error, empty, and recovery states.

FOUNDATION · DECISIONS 01–12

Visual language and everyday interaction

The shared base prevents each module from inventing its own look, hierarchy, or navigation behaviour.

Accepted · 01

Graphite & moss colour system

Use graphite surfaces with moss as the primary accent; light mode uses the matching ink-and-moss family. Moss is never the only success signal—text and icon meaning remain required.

Accepted · 01 implementation contract

Forest Ledger light and dark palette

Use Forest Ledger for the shared platform foundation. Light: base #F7F8F4, surface #FFFFFF, elevated #EEF2EA, primary text #1E261F, secondary text #566258, border #D5DDD1, moss accent #496B43, accent text #F5FAF1, focus #7A5A19. Dark: base #161B17, surface #202820, elevated #2B352B, primary text #EEF2EB, secondary text #B1BAAF, border #3C483C, moss accent #B8D5A9, accent text #142014, focus #E8C66A. Verify all rendered text, component, and focus combinations before release.

Accepted · 02

Follow device on first use

Use the operating-system appearance initially. A stored user override, once provided, takes precedence. Both themes are first-class and must be tested.

Accepted · 03

Poppins as the shared UI voice

Buttons, navigation, labels, and content use Poppins consistently. Tables, schedules, and finance retain compact type, padding, and tabular numerals so personality never costs operational density.

Accepted · 04

Balanced information density

Use comfortable scanning space by default. Dense data surfaces may be compact when the work demands it, without reducing accessible targets.

Accepted · 05

Tiered shape language

Cards, forms, and drawers use soft 8px corners; selected emphasis can be rounder; tables, grids, finance, and schedules stay crisp (0–4px). Pills are reserved for filters and statuses.

Accepted · 06

One clear next action

Each context has one filled primary action. Secondary actions recede; destructive actions are distinct and confirmed.

Accepted · 07

Nearby result plus toast feedback

Show the outcome and recovery close to the action, with a brief cross-app toast when useful. Modals are for irreversible risk, not routine success.

Accepted · 08

Global rail, local workspace

Global navigation changes products; each module owns local navigation. Permissions hide inaccessible destinations rather than creating dead links.

Accepted · 09

Validate on blur and submit

Validate a completed field, then validate all fields on submit. Explain the fix beside the field and move focus to the first invalid field after failed submission.

Accepted · 10

Explain failure or emptiness and offer the next action

Loading, no results, permissions, and request failure are different states. Name the actual state and provide a relevant, reversible recovery path.

Accepted · 11

Context-based task containers

Use drawers for contextual edits, modals for destructive confirmation, and full pages for complex multi-step work. Drawers promote to full screens on small devices.

Accepted · 12

Visible search and active filters

Keep search, current filters, and default sort visible. Advanced panels and saved views are introduced only when data complexity justifies them.

OPERATIONAL WORK · DECISIONS 13–20

Selection, mobile execution, and recovery

These rules make repetitive work fast without hiding consequences or splitting phone work from the authoritative platform.

Accepted · 13

Contextual bulk-action bar

Keep ordinary tables calm. After selection, reveal the exact selected count and available batch actions. On phone, the action bar becomes a reachable sticky bottom area; destructive batches name the affected count.

Accepted · 14

Mobile data: focused workflow where justified

For a different, repeated field role, phone may use its own focused flow. Otherwise prioritise the essential scan and reveal detail on demand. In either case share data, permission, language, and audit state.

Accepted · 15

Focused mobile navigation

Use a limited task-focused bottom navigation inside the shared product model when mobile work is distinct. Keep safe areas, labels, and a small number of destinations; do not invent a disconnected app.

Accepted · 16

Next-task queue as field home

Field workers open on the next actionable task and a short ordered queue. Full schedules remain one navigation step away; scheduling roles retain their desktop calendar workspace.

Accepted · 17

Brief readiness checks

For preparation-dependent tasks, show a short role-specific checklist before start. Do not turn it into a long form; use immediate start for genuinely safe, reversible work.

Accepted · 18

Checklist with visible progress

Repeatable work uses a short checklist and progress count. Use a strict wizard only when sequence or compliance truly requires it. Desktop shows richer context; phone keeps execution focused.

Accepted · 19

Completion confirms, then offers the next task

Confirm the saved outcome and offer the next relevant task without auto-starting it. The completed record remains recoverable from history.

Accepted · 20

Structured exception and recovery

Capture a short reason, actor, timestamp, and valid recovery path rather than an ambiguous “incomplete” state. The same exception state is stored centrally for desktop and phone.

SHARED SYSTEM PATTERNS · DECISIONS 21–33

Data, forms, resilience, access, and time

These decisions are reusable across Booking, Workforce, L+AMS, Payments, Product Manager, POS, ecommerce, and future modules.

Accepted · 21

Status means text + icon + colour

Every status remains understandable without colour. Centralise status names and meanings; modules may change density, not semantic meaning.

Accepted · 22

Grouped sections with one save boundary

Related fields are edited together in one form. Phone opens the same groups as focused edit surfaces. Use a wizard only for genuine dependency chains.

Accepted · 23

Content-shaped skeletons for initial loading

Preserve expected layout while initial data loads, then resolve in place. Clearly distinguish initial loading, refreshing known data, stale cached data, and error.

Accepted · 24

Plain-language errors with targeted retry

Preserve safe filters, location, and entered context; explain impact and offer recovery. Diagnostics are safe support data, not the main message.

Accepted · 25

Empty states name scope and correction

Distinguish “no results for these filters” from “no records exist.” Preserve active filters and provide one relevant corrective action.

Accepted · 26

Contextual destructive confirmation

Name the record and impact, offer the safe alternative, and provide undo when valid. Typed confirmation is reserved for permanent or high-blast-radius actions.

Accepted · 27

Action queue plus supporting metrics

Operational homes lead with work that needs attention, then compact performance context. Executive reporting can use a separate metrics-first view.

Accepted · 28

Context-aware permission visibility

Hide sensitive destinations; explain a relevant restriction inside permitted work. UI visibility is guidance only—routes, functions, and queries still enforce authority server-side.

Accepted · 29

Adaptive cross-device action target density

Use context-adaptive targets: dense desktop data work may use compact controls only when every pointer target meets WCAG’s 24px size-or-spacing rule; comfortable desktop controls use the shared standard size; primary phone task controls are 44px. This keeps schedules, tables, and finance practical without making repeated touch work unreliable.

Accepted · 30

Quiet integrated focus treatment

No external focus ring. The focused component itself changes border and surface contrast so keyboard and assistive users can still locate focus reliably.

Accepted · 31

Context-aware drafts and unsaved-change protection

Retain safe local drafts and ask only when leaving could discard meaningful work. Drafts are not committed records and cannot silently commit financial or destructive decisions.

Accepted · 32

Shared presets plus personal saved views

Store filters, sort, columns, and a name—not copied data. Recheck permissions every run; shared views need ownership and a change policy.

Accepted · 33

Contextual time-zone visibility

Establish the business schedule zone once, then reveal exact or alternate local time only when relevant. Store unambiguous moments plus scheduling zone where audit semantics matter.

INTEGRATION AND GOVERNANCE · DECISIONS 34–50

How the product stays coherent as it grows

These rules connect the UI to auditability, file storage, offline work, product enablement, and shared platform data.

Accepted · 34

Human activity timeline, audit detail on demand

Routine users see an actor/action/time timeline with meaningful before/after context. Authorised reviewers can open the immutable raw audit events; the timeline is a projection, not the source of truth.

Accepted · 35

Contextual evidence capture

Attach files or photos from the work that needs them, then retain them in record history with owner, purpose, uploader, retention, scan status, and permission scope. Phone can promote camera capture; desktop can prioritise existing files.

Accepted · 36

Minimum necessary sensitive data

Show task-relevant context by default; reveal protected fields only for an authorised purpose. Field-level policy applies in APIs, exports, logs, and attachments—not merely the UI.

Accepted · 37

Progressive advanced filtering

Keep common filters visible, then open a focused advanced builder when needed. Translate it into an allow-listed typed query model; saved views store that model, never raw query text.

Accepted · 38

Scoped, queued, secure exports

Review export scope before queuing a durable server job. Notify the requester when a short-lived secure download is ready; snapshot scope, log access, and recheck permission at creation and download.

Accepted · 39

Explicit offline queue and sync state

Allow only explicitly supported local work, display pending changes and network-only limits, then reconcile centrally. A local queue is not permission bypass or final acceptance.

Accepted · 40

Field-level sync conflict resolution

Preserve authored competing values, merge non-conflicting changes, and resolve only the affected field visibly. Use server-side version checks and audit the policy and resolution.

Accepted · 41

Staged import with explicit commit

Upload to staging, map and validate data, preview creates/updates/skips/errors, and require an explicit production commit. Imports need deduplication, idempotency, row results, and an audit record.

Accepted · 42

Resumable module setup

Guide new owners with required/optional setup outcomes and deep links into real settings pages. Completion derives from validated configuration, not a cosmetic tutorial checklist.

Accepted · 43

Contextual help plus deeper learning

Explain the next meaningful action at the point of work, then link to searchable help when needed. Help content is versioned with behaviour and never compensates for bad information architecture.

Accepted · 44

Notification persistence by consequence

Keep outcome near its action, use a brief toast for cross-app awareness, and create a durable notification only for consequential, asynchronous, expiring, or later-actionable events.

Accepted · 45

Canonical money, time, and locale formatting

Store money as minor-unit integer plus ISO currency, and temporal values canonically. Render them through one shared formatter with declared business/user context; official financial surfaces name currency.

Accepted · 46

Visible, explicit workspace switching

Show the active organisation/location whenever it changes data meaning. A deliberate switch refreshes scope and never carries selections or drafts across contexts.

Accepted · 47

Hide unenabled products; recover direct links for owners

Default navigation hides unenabled products. If an eligible owner reaches a direct, stale, or bookmarked link, show a recovery gate to the actual enablement flow; routes and APIs remain gated.

Accepted · 48

Canonical cross-module references

Show an authorised summary and link to the owning customer, staff, payment, or other canonical record. Use stable references rather than copied mutable facts or raw IDs.

Accepted · 49

Contextual plus authorised global search

Everyday search stays within the active module; global command search returns only authorised records, identifies the owning module, and rechecks authority on open.

Accepted · 50

Central shared preferences, local workflow preferences

Appearance, accessibility, and notification choices live in one versioned profile. Calendar view, saved views, map state, and other workflow settings stay in their owning module namespace.

Implementation checklist for every module

  • Use the shared theme tokens and Poppins system; do not invent a private colour or interaction language.
  • Model loading, stale, empty, denied, error, success, destructive, and recovery states before calling a surface complete.
  • Keep desktop and phone previews, handlers, permissions, and audit transitions tied to the same domain state.
  • Test real keyboard focus, touch target size, text contrast, narrow layouts, and the complete failure/recovery path in a browser.
